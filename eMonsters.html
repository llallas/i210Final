Name: El Cuero

Description: El Cuero, meaning "the hide" or "leather", resembles a primitive stingray the most. It has wide pectoral fins and a long, whip-like tail, although an obvious barb is absent. Its eyes are on stalks and its mouth is potentially extendable, like many apparent bottom-feeders. El Cuero ranges from 2-5 feet across and approximately 65 pounds.. Eyewitnesses have also reported seeing a series of razor-sharp claws along the fringes of El Cuero, which the creature uses to secure its prey.

Location: El Cuero apparently hunts in the Chilean glacial Lake Lacar, located in the Andes Mountains.

Danger Level:

10/10 The creature is very good at camouflage and strong.
