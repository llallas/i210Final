Name: Cherufe

A cherufe is a mythical creature in Mapuche mythology, a man-eating, humanoid being made of rock crystals and magma. It is said to inhabit magma pools within volcanoes and is associated with earthquakes and volcanic eruptions. Some cultures, including the Mapuche, worship them, sometimes viewing them as gods or great dragons

Location: It is said that Cherufe inhabit the magma pools found deep within Chilean volcanoes and are the source of earthquakes and volcanic eruptions.
